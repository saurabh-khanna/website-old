Biography

I am a doctoral student in Educational Policy at Stanford University. I intend to combine insights from network analysis, machine learning, and game theory to understand aspects reshaping education in developing nations. I often ruminate about the fairness of knowledge recommendations (vis-à-vis recommender systems) and possible ways to balance knowledge relevance with knowledge diversity.

I also have a keen interest in utilizing a network science perspective to understand issues faced by stakeholders in education who have been forcibly displaced on account of emergencies. To this effect, some of us kickstarted an interest group on education in emergencies at Stanford to focus on aspects of education in crisis situations wherein the larger society undergoes a striking structural displacement.

I am equally fond of exploring unorthodox films from diverse cultures, cooking on drowsy weekends, chai, and tiramisu.

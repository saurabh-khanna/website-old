Biography

As a doctoral candidate at Stanford, I study social and economic networks in education. I intend to combine insights from network analysis and machine learning to understand aspects reshaping education in developing nations. I often ruminate about the fairness of knowledge access (vis-à-vis search engine bias) and possible ways to balance knowledge relevance with knowledge diversity. To this effect, I am building Sonder – an open-source and nonprofit web search platform that dynamically tackles (evaluates and reduces) biases in search results.

I also have a keen interest in utilizing a network science perspective to understand issues faced by stakeholders in education who have been forcibly displaced on account of emergencies. A few of us banded together to kickstart an interest group on education in emergencies at Stanford to focus on aspects of education in crisis situations wherein the larger society undergoes a striking structural displacement.

I am equally fond of exploring unorthodox films from diverse cultures, cooking on drowsy weekends, chai, and tiramisu.
